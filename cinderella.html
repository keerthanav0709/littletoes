Cinderella

Once upon a time, there lived a kind and beautiful girl named Cinderella. After her mother passed away, her father remarried a woman with two daughters who were mean and jealous of Cinderella's beauty and goodness.

When her father died, Cinderella was forced to become a servant in her own home. She cooked, cleaned, and slept by the fireplace, which made her clothes dirty and her face covered in ash — hence the name “Cinderella.”

One day, the king held a grand ball to find a bride for his son. Cinderella wanted to go, but her stepmother wouldn’t allow it. While her stepsisters went to the ball, Cinderella was left crying at home.

Suddenly, a Fairy Godmother appeared! With a wave of her wand, she turned Cinderella’s ragged dress into a beautiful gown, a pumpkin into a carriage, and mice into horses. But there was one rule — she must return before midnight, or the magic would disappear.

At the ball, the prince was enchanted by Cinderella’s grace and beauty. They danced all night. But as the clock struck midnight, Cinderella ran away, leaving behind only a glass slipper.

The prince searched far and wide. Every woman tried on the slipper, but it fit no one — until he reached Cinderella’s home. The slipper fit her perfectly, and her true identity was revealed. The prince married her, and they lived happily ever after.

Moral: Kindness and inner beauty will always shine through and be rewarded in the end.
